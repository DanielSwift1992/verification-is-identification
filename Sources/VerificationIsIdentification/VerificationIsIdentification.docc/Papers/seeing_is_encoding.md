# Seeing Is Encoding (Sensor)

A sensor does not hand over the world. It hands over an image in a smaller carrier, and the image is all a reader downstream can ever compare. This paper derives what follows for light: a beam is a finite structure, matter is a presented law over it, the eye is an encoding that can hold two beams to one image, and a prism is a presented separator that hands the lost distinction back. The claim, pinned: seeing is encoding — identification stops at the image unless a separator is presented, and with a stated basis the whole of ray transport becomes exact, counted arithmetic. The lattice encodes the two claims (`Seeing.swift`); this is the paper the encoding was written against.

The claims are not about light alone. Any sensor over any structure encodes, any encoding with a kernel forgets, and any presented separator restores. Light is the worked instance because physics fixes every piece: the basis by transition rules, the projection by the eye, the separator by glass.

## §0. Derivation

No new postulate. The encoding axis already bounds what a reader can reach (V=I §5.24), a beam is a finite structure (Existence Is Finite), and matter acting on light is a presented law (Dynamics Is Substitution). Everything below applies those three to one instance.

## §1. The beam

**SiE1 (Light is a structure, and matter writes its alphabet).** A beam is a stated finite basis of spectral lines with one brightness level per line: a finite tree, whole. The basis is not chosen freely. A line is an allowed transition of some substance — the selection rule admits it or it does not exist — so the alphabet of light is written by matter, and a forbidden line cannot enter a beam: it has no type. A continuous spectrum is not a structure here; it is the standing possibility of finer bases, placed outside, never a finished individual.

## §2. Matter is a passive law

**SiE2 (A filter can only narrow).** Passive matter is a dictionary of rules over beams, and every rule's output level is the input's or the floor: absorption is compress. The class is closed by construction — the dictionary contains no rule that raises a level or adds a line, so "a filter that amplifies" is not a rejected program but an unwritable one. Emission is the one expand, and it is bounded by §1: a substance emits exactly its allowed transitions.

## §3. The eye forgets

**SiE3 (Metamerism is |S| > 1 at the image).** An eye reads some slots of the beam and drops the rest, then sums what it reads into few channels. Both moves have kernels: a dropped slot is an invisible coordinate, and a sum forgets its split. So two distinct beams can land on one image — a metameric pair — and the pair is presentable: state two beams differing in a dropped slot, and the sharing of their image is a checkable identity. Downstream of the eye, the image is the whole evidence, and identification stops: |S| > 1, by the encoding, not by any fault of the reader. **[solid — ``EncodingForgets``, witnessed by the pair and its shared image]**

## §4. The prism recalls

**SiE4 (A presented separator restores identification).** A prism reads every slot: each line lands at its own place, so the spread image is the beam itself, and any two distinct beams land apart. Routing the metameric pair through the prism returns the lost distinction — the same pair that shares an eye-image refuses to share a prism-image, and the refusal names the slot where the twins differ. The separator is presented, never searched for: the author states the instrument, the judge checks it parts the pair. **[solid — ``SeparatorIdentifies``]**

## §5. Transport is counted

**SiE5 (Exactness on a stated basis).** A scene is a finite graph of sources, matter, and screens. A ray is a path; a chain of filters is a written chain, its depth in the type; and the image at a screen is a finite sum over finitely many paths. Nothing is sampled and nothing converges — the sum is exact, because both classical infinities were closed by statement: the continuous spectrum by the finite basis, the unbounded recursion by the counted depth. Sampling in spectral rendering is the tax on an unstated basis. With the basis stated, the tax is zero.

**SiE6 (Reversal where nothing is erased).** A passive rule that erases nothing reverses by swapping its pattern and its template — the reversibility of the ray path is the reversal theorem of presented laws, inherited, not re-proved. A killing filter erases, and honestly does not reverse: what reached the floor carries no record of its height.

## §6. Mixing before the edge

**SiE7 (The sum of light is a monoid, and the screen is not).** Mixing beams is slotwise addition over the basis: commutative, associative, with darkness as the unit. The linear part of a sensor respects it. The display's edge does not — its response curve is nonlinear, so the image of a mix is not the mix of the images. Mixing after the edge is the standard mistake; mixing before it is exact by construction. The edge is where numbers belong, and where they stop being algebra.

## §7. Distance

**SiE8 (Colour difference is structural distance).** The distance between two beams is the structural distance between their trees, and it is positive for every metameric pair: what the eye calls equal, the structure counts apart. Perceptual difference is a property of the encoding; structural difference is a property of the light. The two agree only where the encoding is faithful, and the gap between them is the measured loss of the eye.

## §8. Statement

Seeing is encoding. A beam is a finite structure whose alphabet matter writes. Passive matter narrows it, emission extends it along allowed transitions, and both are presented laws. The eye projects the beam into a smaller image and thereby forgets: metameric pairs exist and are presentable. A prism, presented, tells every pair apart. On a stated basis, transport is a counted, exact sum, reversal holds wherever nothing is erased, and mixing is a monoid until the display's edge. What the image cannot decide, the structure decides — and the structure is what this notation writes.
