# Light Is a Ruler (Measure)

A unit of measure is not a thing kept in a vault. It is the tick of a law with one survivor, and measuring is counting those ticks along a path. This paper derives measurement from the chain already standing: the standard as a type, the ruler as a homomorphism, the metric axioms as theorems of transport, phase as a remainder, the glint as a path to oneself, and calibration as a distance computed before either clock ticks. The claim, pinned: light is a ruler because a presented law with |S| = 1 is a unit, and a path is a number waiting to be read. The lattice encodes the two claims (`Ruler.swift`); this is the paper the encoding was written against.

The second is already defined this way: a stated count of periods of one atomic transition. The metre is the path light walks in a stated fraction of it. Metrology arrived here by shedding instances — a kept bar wears, a written law does not — and that shedding is the same move this notation makes for software: the standard is a type, never a stored value.

## §0. Derivation

No new postulate. A path carries a length (Program Is Path), a presented law is deterministic (Dynamics Is Substitution), an encoding can forget and a presented separator restores (Seeing Is Encoding), and distance is a count of distinctions (Distance Is Cost). Measurement is those four, composed.

## §1. The standard is a type

**LiR1 (A unit is a tick with one survivor).** A standard is a presented law whose repeating tick admits exactly one trajectory: deterministic, so any two realizations of the tick are the same tick, and the unit reproduces wherever the law can be written. A pendulum fails the bar — its period rides free parameters, |S| > 1, and the unit drifts with the instance. An atomic transition passes: the levels are discrete, the tick is fixed by the law alone. An artifact standard — the kept bar — is an instance: singular, stored, wearing. The history of standards is the move from the instance to the type. **[solid — ``StandardIsType``]**

## §2. The ruler is a homomorphism

**LiR2 (Measuring is counting).** To measure a path with a unit is to tally the unit's ticks along it: a reading, never a making. Where paths concatenate, the tallies add. So a ruler is a homomorphism from the monoid of paths to the naturals, and the arithmetic of measurement — sums of lengths, differences, comparisons — is inherited from addition, not postulated. **[solid — ``MeasureIsCount``]**

## §3. The metric axioms are theorems of transport

**LiR3 (Distance from light).** Define the distance between two points as the tally of the shortest light path between them. The axioms follow from transport. Identity: the empty path counts zero. Symmetry: every edge is walkable both ways at the same count — the reversibility of the ray path, inherited from the reversal theorem of presented laws. The triangle inequality: paths concatenate, and a shortest path never exceeds a concatenation. Nothing is assumed of "space"; the metric is a property of counted light.

**LiR4 (Constancy is the invariance of the form).** The claim "light is constant" is, here, exact and modest: the tick is one law on every edge of the scene, and the form of a presented law never moves along a trajectory. So the ruler does not depend on which line measured, or when. Constancy is not a postulate about nature. It is what "presented whole" already means.

## §4. Phase is a remainder

**LiR5 (Quotient and remainder are two readings).** A length against a unit is a quotient and a remainder: length = q·U + r. Time-of-flight ranging reads q. Interferometry reads r — fringe counting is the comparison of remainders, and the remainder lives in a finite ring, no wave amplitude required. Read together, the pair (q, r) is a positional numeral in a mixed radix: the vernier, the heterodyne, the two-scale rangefinder are one construction. A measurement of any precision is a walk down the divisions of a tick — the same walk-shape a colour takes down the divisions of brightness. Measure and colour read by one form.

## §5. The glint is a path to oneself

**LiR6 (The Seeing pair, a second time).** A matte surface scatters alike into every edge: it forgets which side its light came from. Two lightings from opposite sides land on one matte image — directional metamerism, an encoding with a kernel. A mirror keeps the direction whole, and the same two lightings land apart. This is the Seeing pair instantiated a second time: the matte wall is the eye of directions, the mirror is their prism. One pair, two worlds.

What follows is the observer's own place in the image. A diffuse patch belongs to the scene: it does not move when the viewer moves. A glint is a mirror path whose type contains the viewer's node, so it moves with the viewer — the glint is the observer, written into the image. And the glint measures: a mirror round trip is the distance paired with itself, so the distance is the half of a tallied glint path, and the halving is a checkable identity, not a computation. Ranging by light — time-of-flight on the quotient, interference on the remainder — is reading the length of one's own path.

## §6. Calibration before the tick

**LiR7 (Standards compare at rest).** A presented law carries a norm and a distance, so two standards are comparable before either runs: calibration is a distance between laws, computed from their texts. A type does not age — there is no kept body to wear — so the drift that haunted artifact standards has nothing to ride. What remains at the edge is the instrument: hardware is an instance, and its agreement with the written law stays an empirical duty, named, not proved.

## §7. Statement

Light is a ruler. A unit is the tick of a presented law with one survivor, so the standard is a type and needs no keeping. Measuring is tallying ticks along a path, so the ruler is a homomorphism and the metric axioms are theorems of transport. Phase is a remainder, precision is a walk down divisions, and the finest measurements read quotient and remainder together. A matte wall forgets direction and a mirror restores it — the Seeing pair again — so the glint carries the observer, and ranging is reading the length of one's own path, halved by an identity. Calibration compares laws at rest. What measurement needed, the chain already had.

## Scope

Claimed: the standard, the count, the metric, the remainder reading, the glint, and calibration, over stated finite bases and counted paths. Not claimed: relativity's geometry, the quantum phase as amplitude (only the remainder is taken), the noise of physical instruments, and any drift of the medium — the hardware stays an instance at the edge, and the continuous stays placed as potential.
