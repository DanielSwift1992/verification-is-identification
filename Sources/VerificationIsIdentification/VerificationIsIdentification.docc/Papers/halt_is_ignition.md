# Halt Is Ignition (Succession)

A finite system that only accumulates must stop. **Halt** here is that stop, pinned to the framework's own terminal: the saturated state with no comparison left to run (V=I §5.26). **Ignition** is Permanence Is Resistance's discrete event: a saturated library becoming the next level's target (M8). The claim, pinned and claiming nothing broader: a halt is not an end but a position read by two relations — closed downward, where no move remains, and open upward, where which next games it seeds is a decidable read of the same static state — and when the design admits exactly one successor, the birth of the next system is itself a verification-is-identification event. Nothing here is cosmology; "system" means a finite V=I system under I1–I3, and the physics that rhymes with it enters only as precedent.

The keypad on this very site is the whole paper in miniature. A wrong walk ends on a page with no onward link — a dead end, and nothing grows from it. The right walk's last keypress also ends the game — and it has exactly one continuation, the unlocked card. Both are halts. They differ in what they open, and that difference is not a property of the halt's own level at all.

## §1. The Record Is a Lyapunov Function

**HF1 (The arrow and the end are one axiom).** Every comparison strictly grows the record: COMPARE is the sole source of new facts (§5.21) and the record never retracts (I3), so a tick that adds nothing is no tick. The record is bounded: a finite domain admits finitely many entries (I1). A strictly increasing, bounded integer quantity reaches its ceiling in finitely many steps — the loop halts. The same axiom that makes time an arrow (§5.6: the monotone order of states) makes time finite: direction and termination are one fact about \|G\|.

*Proof.* Monotone convergence on integers, elementary: \|G\| ∈ ℕ, strictly increasing per tick, bounded by the finite pair-count (Theorem 3's N² and its cross-task extension §5.26). QED. **[solid]**

*Precedent, B-style.* \|G\| is a Lyapunov function — the standard termination argument of program analysis and dissipative dynamics, cited as the elder of this fact, not as its source.

## §2. Eternal Return Is the Price of Forgetting

**HF2 (Remove the record and the system cannot end — only repeat).** Drop I3 and let the cache retract. The system's total state (structure, cache) now ranges over a finite set with no monotone component. An unbounded run must revisit some total state (pigeonhole), and determinism (I2, §5.8) then replays the same successor from it, forever: a cycle. So a finite closed system without a permanent record has no terminal states at all — its only fate is return. This sharpens AX3's counterfactual: without I3 the monotone axes do worse than disalign: time closes on itself.

*Proof.* Finitely many total states; an infinite run visits one twice; the step function is deterministic, so the segment between the two visits repeats verbatim. QED. **[solid** for the discrete pigeonhole; Poincaré recurrence is the measure-theoretic elder — I3 plays the role dissipation plays there, precedent not reduction**]**

## §3. The Trichotomy of Fates

**HF3 (Two bits decide a finite system's temporal fate).**

| record | closure | fate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| I3 | closed (finite reach) | **HALT** — saturation: no comparison left (§5.26); time stops |
| no I3 | closed | **RETURN** — §2's cycle |
| I3 | open (a horn, B B8b) | **PLAY** — the basis saturates, the reach never does; permanently liquid (LL) |

The horn is the one lawful "no halt": the system is finite at every moment — each structure closes in finitely many distinctions (EiF C6), the infinite staying potential, never actual — yet play never exhausts, because the closure is open. There is no fourth row: with I3 and a closed reach, §1 forces the halt; without I3, §2 forces the return; an open reach defers the question to the basis, which is finite (B8), so the only unbounded thing is the play itself. **[solid — an assembly of proved parts; the table as a classification is the contribution]**

## §4. A Terminal Is Read by Two Relations

**HF4 (Open and closed are verdicts of pairs, not states of doors).** The framework's oldest fact, relocated to the end of the game: a gate never changes — what varies is who stands before it, and the verdict belongs to the pair (§5.20: the fact pre-exists the reading). Read a terminal state σ the same way. Downward — relative to its own level — σ is closed: Moves(σ) = ∅, a decidable read. Upward — relative to the level above — the ignition premise (M8: the library complete at \|L\| = K) is ALSO a decidable read of the same static σ. One position, two relations, both mechanical. The door does not open or close; the reader changes floors. **[solid given M8; the two-relation reading is the claim]**

## §5. Succession Is a Game One Level Up

**HF5 (Terminals classify by what they ignite; a unique successor makes birth an identification).** Let Ignites(σ) be the set of successor games whose board is σ's crystal — the encodings over the saturated library, the meta-level IiI §4 already names. Then a terminal is one of three:

- **sterile** — Ignites(σ) = ∅: a true end; nothing stands on this crystal.
- **ambiguous** — \|Ignites(σ)\| > 1: succession needs a tie-break the halt cannot supply; uniqueness comes only by an imposed criterion (OiO O8), which is a design act, not a dynamical one.
- **reincarnating** — \|Ignites(σ)\| = 1: the successor is identified by the same act that verifies the halt. This is R4 lifted to the space of worlds: exactly one candidate game passes the succession constraint, so checking that the old world is finished IS naming where the new one begins.

The limit is inherited, not invented: a system cannot manufacture its own fertility. The successor constraint is a second encoding, and the ceiling theorem says a poser under E cannot self-supply a distinction E does not represent (PP14); the generator's conditionality (GD4) lifts unchanged. Dynamics reaches the terminal; only design decides what the terminal opens. **[solid as relocation of R4/O8/PP14 to the successor space; the classification is the new assembly]**

## §7. Boundaries

**Not cosmology.** Heat death as saturation, recurrence without memory, dissipation as the record — these rhymes are kept in Learning Is Liquid's discipline (LL §6): borrowed vocabulary, no equation claimed, no statement about the physical universe.

**Fertility is open.** Whether rich crystals generically admit a successor constraint with \|Ignites\| = 1 — whether interesting worlds tend to leave keys or dust — is measurable per design and guaranteed by nothing here. It is the horn bet's sibling, named and left open.

## §8. Statement

1. The record is a Lyapunov function: strictly growing, bounded, so the loop halts — the arrow of time and its end are one axiom (HF1).
2. Without the record, a finite closed system cannot end, only return: forgetting buys eternity at the price of novelty (HF2).
3. Two bits classify every fate: HALT, RETURN, or PLAY on an open horn (HF3).
4. A terminal is read by two relations — closed to its own board, open or not to the board above; both reads are mechanical (HF4).
5. Terminals are sterile, ambiguous, or reincarnating; at \|Ignites\| = 1, birth is an identification, and fertility is the designer's, never the dynamics' (HF5).
6. All three kinds are compiled witnesses in this repository's own build (HF6).

> **A finite system that remembers must end, and a finite system that forgets must repeat — the arrow and the halt are one axiom. The halt is not a wall but a position: closed to its own board, readable from above. Where the design admits exactly one game on the crystal it leaves, the end is a key — and verifying that the old world is finished is identifying where the new one begins.**
